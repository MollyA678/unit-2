About This Map

This map is inspired by my Intro to East Asian Civilizations coursework this semester. As I've been learning about the history and development of major cities across East and Southeast Asia, it has been challenging to keep track of how urban populations have shifted over time and how historical events affected urban development. I created this interactive map to help me visualize these changes, allowing me to explore population trends from ancient times to the present. Use the slider to view different time periods, and select a city from the dropdown to locate it. Many of these cities stay at zero population for a long time. Whether this is because they were actually not inhabited yet, or because some values at worldcitypop.com were missing data is unclear. There are likely cases of both. Places like Jakarta or Manila only are denoted as urban centers under their current names after colonizers arrived and opened trade ports there. You will see these cities grow rapidly in the 20th century. By contrast, cities like Xi'an and Beijing have been inhabited since ancient times, and thus, show early population growth. This map will hopefully inspire you to learn more about the factors that drove urbanization through East/Southeast Asian history, including trade, colonization, industrialization, and more. As you explore the map, consider:

  • How do different cities compare in when they start growing and the rate at which they expand?
  • What historical events or economic factors might explain the trends in a given city?
  • Are there patterns distributed across national borders? Along coasts? Subregions?
